Overview of the Cessna150E

The Cessna 150 is a high-wing two seater aircraft that served as a trainer for many students. In particular, the Cessna 150 model E was produced as the variant for the 1965 model year. Among the Cessna 150E's unique features are the large window in the rear, straight tail and manual flaps; the rudder on the straight tail was actually more effective than newer versions of the Cessna 150 and is more effective when it comes to spin training. The manual flaps have a 40 degree setting which means you can land on a dime.

Specifications:

  • Continental O-200 4 cylinder 100HP air-cooled engine
  • Climb-rate of 500 feet per minute at sea level
  • Absolute ceiling of 10,500 feet MSL (personal experience, not from pilot operator's handbook)
  • Cruise at 90 knots (personal experience, not from pilot operator's handbook
  • Fuel consumption of 5 gallons per hour with lean mixture
